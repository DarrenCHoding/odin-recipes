Rendang

Description

Rendang is a rich and tender beef dish slow-cooked in coconut milk and a blend of aromatic spices until the liquid reduces into a thick, dark, deeply flavorful sauce. Originating from the Minangkabau region of West Sumatra, it's often considered one of the most flavorful dishes in the world, balancing spicy, savory, and slightly sweet notes.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef chuck, cut into chunks
  • 800 ml coconut milk
  • 4 shallots
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 3 red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 3 cm ginger
  • 3 cm galangal
  • 2 cm turmeric
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, bruised
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 turmeric leaves (optional)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Blend shallots, garlic, chilies, ginger, galangal, and turmeric into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot and sauté the spice paste until fragrant.
  3. Add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and turmeric leaves; stir well.
  4. Add the beef chunks and cook until the meat changes color.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and tamarind paste, stir to combine.
  6. Simmer on low heat, stirring occasionally, for 2–3 hours until the sauce thickens and darkens.
  7. Season with salt and continue cooking until the oil separates and the meat is tender.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.
