Oxtail Soup

Description

Sop Buntut is a rich Indonesian oxtail soup made by slow-simmering oxtail with aromatic spices and vegetables until the meat is fall-off-the-bone tender and the broth is deeply flavorful. It's a hearty, comforting dish often served with steamed rice or crusty bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 2 liters water
  • 2 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 2 potatoes, cut into chunks
  • 1 tomato, quartered
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 2 scallions, chopped
  • 4 shallots
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg, grated
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fried shallots, for garnish

Steps

  1. Boil the oxtail in water for a few minutes, then discard the water to remove impurities.
  2. Return the oxtail to the pot with fresh water and bring to a boil, then simmer.
  3. Blend or crush the shallots and garlic, then sauté in oil until fragrant.
  4. Add the sautéed aromatics, nutmeg, and white pepper to the simmering oxtail.
  5. Continue simmering for 2–3 hours until the oxtail is tender.
  6. Add carrots and potatoes, cooking until softened.
  7. Add tomato and celery, and season with salt to taste.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with scallions and fried shallots.
