Ayam Penyet

Description

Ayam Penyet is a popular Indonesian dish of fried chicken that is "smashed" or pressed to tenderize it and let it soak up the flavors of a fiery sambal (chili paste). It's typically served with steamed rice, fresh cucumber, and cabbage.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken pieces (thigh or drumstick)
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil for deep frying

For the sambal

  • 8 bird's eye chilies
  • 3 red chilies
  • 3 shallots
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken with crushed garlic, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Deep fry the marinated chicken until golden brown and cooked through.
  3. For the sambal, boil or fry the chilies, shallots, garlic, and tomatoes until softened.
  4. Grind or pound the boiled ingredients together with sugar and salt into a coarse paste.
  5. Place the fried chicken on a mortar or plate and gently smash it, then top generously with sambal.
  6. Serve with steamed rice, sliced cucumber, and cabbage.
